## 1. Executive Summary

The chemical processing plant experienced an unexpected shutdown due to a 6-hour production halt caused by an unexpected loss of pressure in the coolant circulation system. An investigation revealed that a major leak in one of the primary coolant supply pipelines resulted from advanced corrosion and rust buildup. This corrosion was due to a failure in the facility's regular preventive maintenance schedule for aging infrastructure. This RCA report aims to provide a comprehensive analysis of the root cause and recommendations for corrective and preventive actions.

## 2. Detailed Analysis

### Problem Statement
The chemical processing plant experienced an unexpected shutdown resulting in a 6-hour production halt due to a loss of pressure in the coolant circulation system, which was caused by a major leak in one of the primary coolant supply pipelines.

### 5 Whys Breakdown
#### Why 1: Why did the chemical processing plant experience an unexpected shutdown resulting in a 6-hour production halt?
- **Why 2: Why did the coolant circulation system lose pressure and fail to maintain safe operating temperatures?**
  - **Why 3: Why did the primary coolant supply pipeline develop a major leak?**
    - **Why 4: Why did the advanced corrosion and rust buildup occur in the primary coolant supply pipeline?**
      - **Why 5: Why did routine inspection and maintenance checks fail to identify and address the deteriorating condition of the pipeline, as the facility did not follow a regular, enforced preventive maintenance schedule for aging infrastructure?**
        - **Conclusion:**
          - Routine inspection and maintenance checks failed to identify and address the deteriorating condition of the primary coolant supply pipeline due to advanced corrosion and rust buildup, as the facility did not follow a regular, enforced preventive maintenance schedule for aging infrastructure.

### Root Cause
The root cause of the unexpected shutdown and 6-hour production halt is that routine inspection and maintenance checks failed to identify and address the deteriorating condition of the primary coolant supply pipeline due to advanced corrosion and rust buildup, as the facility did not follow a regular, enforced preventive maintenance schedule for aging infrastructure.

88.8%

## 3. Corrective and Preventive Actions

### Immediate Actions
1. **Conduct a Comprehensive Inspection:** Immediately inspect all primary coolant supply pipelines for any signs of corrosion or rust buildup.
2. **Implement Temporary Measures:** Install temporary cooling systems to ensure uninterrupted production until the primary coolant system is repaired.
3. **Review and Update Maintenance Schedule:** Develop and implement an updated, enforced preventive maintenance schedule for all aging infrastructure.

### Long-term Actions
1. **Enhanced Inspection Protocols:** Develop and enforce enhanced inspection protocols for all primary coolant supply pipelines, including more frequent and detailed inspections.
2. **Regular Maintenance:** Establish a regular, enforced maintenance schedule that includes targeted inspections and repairs for all aging infrastructure components.
3. **Training and Awareness:** Provide training for all maintenance personnel on the importance of regular inspections and preventive maintenance to ensure compliance.

## 4. Recommendations and Follow-up

### Process Improvements
- Integrate regular inspection and preventive maintenance into the daily operations and maintenance processes.
- Ensure all maintenance tasks are documented and reviewed periodically to identify areas for improvement.

### Monitoring
- Monitor the system for any recurring issues and address them promptly.
- Conduct periodic reviews of the preventive maintenance schedule and update it as necessary.

### Review Schedule
- Conduct a comprehensive review of the preventive maintenance schedule every six months.
- Include a stakeholder review process to ensure the maintenance schedule is effective and aligned with facility needs.

By implementing these corrective and preventive actions, the facility can minimize the risk of future unexpected shutdowns and ensure the safety and integrity of its primary coolant supply pipelines.
